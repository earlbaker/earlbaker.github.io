Churchill

26/04/11 20:33 Filed in: History
Admirers of Winston Churchill gathered recently in Charleston, South Carolina. It was actually not because it was the 150th anniversary of the shot at Fort Sumter which started the Civil War, which was coincidental, but because when Churchill was hit by a car in New York in 1932. (he looked the wrong way the same way we do when we go to Britain) Bernard Baruch offered him the opportunity to recuperate at the Hobcaw Barony near Charleston. Baruch had assembled 16 plantations on the Charleston estuary as a hunting preserve and it was a great chance for Churchill to regain his strength in the home there.


Attending the conference was Churchill’s great grandson Randolph, shown here with me and with Allen Packwood, Director of the Churchill archives at Churchill College, Cambridge. While most assembled historians and Churchill experts agree one cannot project the views and attitudes of Churchill from the past on to later situations, in any exact way, it is generally agreed and I agree that his message of gathering and analyzing intelligence, preparing for challenges to democracy that may arise in the future, and a concern for the health of the private economy are all matters at issue today.



We are fortunate that the Churchill Centre keeps the memory alive of Winston Churchill, the man who led England through its tortuous year alone keeping Western Civilization from crumbling before Hitler and the Nazis, and brings together people who want to know more about him, his works, and his legacy.
